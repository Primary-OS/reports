Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 9, 2026

Varick is a Chrome extension that enhances research workflows. This policy explains what data the extension accesses, how it is used, and how it is stored.

Data We Collect

Varick does not collect, transmit, or store any data on servers operated by us. All data remains on your device or is sent directly to third-party APIs that you configure with your own credentials.

Data Stored Locally

  • API credentials — Stored in Chrome's local storage on your device. Never sent to our servers.
  • Session state — Temporary operational state stored locally and cleared automatically after use.
  • User preferences — Settings and toggles stored locally.

Data Sent to Third Parties

When you use Varick, your input may be sent directly from your browser to third-party AI services using API keys you provide. Varick does not proxy, log, or intercept this traffic. No data is routed through our infrastructure.

Website Content Accessed

The extension reads content you have entered in supported web applications to provide context for its features. This content is only processed locally or sent to APIs you have configured.

Data We Do Not Collect

  • No personally identifiable information
  • No browsing history or web activity
  • No analytics, telemetry, or usage tracking
  • No cookies or cross-site tracking

Data Sharing

We do not sell, transfer, or share any user data with third parties. Data is only transmitted to services you have explicitly configured.

Data Retention

All locally stored data can be cleared at any time via the extension's settings or by uninstalling the extension.

Changes to This Policy

If we update this policy, the revised version will be posted at this URL with an updated date.
